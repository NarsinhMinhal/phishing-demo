🎭 What a Phishing Email Looks Like

Inbox — demo@youremail.com
From: security-alert@securebank-verify.net
Reply-To: no-reply@securebank.com

🔍 Red Flags — What to Look For

1

Suspicious Sender Domain

The email appears to be from "securebank-verify.net" — NOT "securebank.com". Attackers register similar-looking domains. Always check the full email address, not just the display name.

2

Mismatched Reply-To

The Reply-To address differs from the From address. This is a classic spoofing technique to make the email look legitimate while replies go to the attacker.

3

Artificial Urgency & Fear

"Account suspended in 24 hours!" is a pressure tactic designed to make you act before you think. Legitimate banks never threaten immediate account closure via email.

4

Vague Greeting

"Dear Valued Customer" instead of your real name. Your actual bank would use your full name since they have it on file. Generic greetings are a phishing signal.

5

Suspicious Call-to-Action Link

The "Verify Now" button links to a fake page. Hover over links before clicking — in real phishing emails, the URL shown won't match the actual destination.

6

Fake Legitimacy Signals

Professional logo, copyright footer, privacy policy links, and physical address — these are all easily copied and pasted to make fake emails look official.
